The Challenge: Signal Clipping

Conventional ADCs suffer from a limited Dynamic Range (DR). When an input signal (like a powerful radar pulse) exceeds the ADC's voltage ceiling, the signal "clips," leading to permanent data loss and severe distortion.

In modern environments—filled with multi-standard receivers and wideband interference—maintaining high fidelity without saturation is a critical bottleneck for signal processing.

The Modulo Solution

Instead of letting the signal saturate, our architecture applies a modulo operation in the analog domain.

  • Signal Folding: When the signal exceeds the DR, it is "folded" back into the ADC's range via a feedback mechanism.
  • Trigger Mechanism: Custom circuitry detects threshold crossings and activates a voltage generator to add/subtract the necessary offset.
  • Virtual Dynamic Range: This allows a low-range ADC to effectively capture high-amplitude signals, which are then digitally reconstructed.

Target Applications

Passive Radar

Detecting targets using existing commercial signals without clipping.

Software Defined Radio (SDR)

Adapting to massive signal variations across multi-standard bands.

Biomedical Instrumentation

Resolving tiny biological signals in the presence of large DC offsets or noise.
